How it works

Three rules. That's the whole thing.

1

Eat under, bank it

If you ate under your daily target yesterday, the difference goes into your weekly bank. Up to a cap so it does not encourage starving.

2

Eat over, spend it

If you go over today, the surplus comes out of your bank first. Your weekly average stays on track.

3

Resets every week

The bank zeros out at the start of each cycle. No carrying debt across weeks. No banking forever.

Why it exists

Daily targets don't match how people eat.

You eat lighter on Tuesday. Friday is a long dinner with friends. A rigid 2000 kcal cap punishes you for both. Either you stress out about the dinner, or you give up entirely on Monday.

Calorie banking lets the math match reality. Your weekly average is what matters. Daily fluctuation is fine, as long as you're roughly on track.

  • Configurable weekly cap so the bank can't get abused
  • Today is never banked or spent — only past completed days
  • Bank balance shown on the home screen as a chip
