# TrackKcal — All Features

## Free Features

### 1. Food Logging
Log what you eat by typing it out or taking a photo. The AI breaks it down into calories, protein, carbs, and fat automatically. You don't need to search a database or scan barcodes. Just say "chicken rice and salad" and it figures out the rest.

Limited to 3 meals per day on the free plan.

**Why it matters:** Most people quit calorie tracking because logging is tedious. Natural language input removes that friction. You describe food the way you'd tell a friend, not the way a database expects it.

---

### 2. Calorie Counter
See your total calories for the day, how much you've eaten, and how much is left. The main number on the Home screen updates in real time as you log.

**Why it matters:** Without a clear daily number, calorie tracking is guesswork. This gives you one number to focus on.

---

### 3. Macro Tracking
Track protein, carbs, and fat alongside calories. Each macro shows current intake vs target with color-coded progress. Swipe the card on the Home screen to switch between the calorie view and the macro view.

**Why it matters:** Calories alone don't tell the full story. Someone eating 2,000 calories of pure sugar has a very different body composition outcome than someone eating 2,000 calories with adequate protein. Macros fill that gap.

---

### 4. Goal Setting
Set your daily calorie target and macro split. The app walks you through a setup flow that considers your age, weight, height, activity level, and whether you want to lose, gain, or maintain weight. It calculates a recommended target and lets you adjust it.

**Why it matters:** A target without context is just a number someone picked. The calculator gives it a foundation based on actual physiology.

---

### 5. Weight Tracker
Log your weight over time. See it on a chart. The app tracks your starting weight, current weight, and target weight. It shows how much you've lost or gained and your overall trend.

**Why it matters:** Daily weight fluctuates due to water, sodium, and digestion. A trend line cuts through the noise and shows actual progress.

---

### 6. Exercise Logging
Type what exercise you did and the app estimates calories burned. "Ran 30 minutes" or "walked 5km" gets parsed into a calorie estimate.

**Why it matters:** Exercise affects your daily energy balance. Logging it gives a more complete picture of your day.

---

### 7. Photo Food Recognition
Take a photo of your meal or upload one from your gallery. The AI identifies the foods, estimates portions, and logs the nutrition breakdown.

**Why it matters:** Sometimes you don't know what's in a dish, especially when eating out. A photo is faster than typing and often more accurate for complex meals.

---

### 8. Health App Connections
Connect to Apple Health on iOS or Health Connect on Android to sync steps and calories burned from your watch or phone.

**Why it matters:** Manual exercise logging is one more thing to remember. Auto-sync from your wearable makes it passive.

---

### 9. Basic Notifications
Set reminders for breakfast, lunch, and dinner. The app nudges you if you haven't logged by your chosen time.

**Why it matters:** The biggest barrier to tracking isn't difficulty, it's forgetting. A well-timed reminder at noon catches the lunch you were about to skip logging.

---

### 10. Streak Tracking
See how many consecutive days you've logged food. Streak freezes (2 per month) protect your streak if you miss a day. The streak card shows your current count and longest streak.

**Why it matters:** Streaks create accountability. Missing one day doesn't break the habit if you have a freeze available. This reduces the "I already missed yesterday so why bother today" spiral.

---

### 11. Referral Program
Share your referral code with friends. When they sign up, you earn 3 free premium days per referral.

**Why it matters:** Users get to try premium features without paying. The app grows through word of mouth instead of ads.

---

### 12. App Walkthrough
An interactive tutorial that explains the main features when you first open the app. Available anytime from Settings under "How it Works."

**Why it matters:** New users who don't understand the app in the first 60 seconds leave and don't come back. The walkthrough prevents that.

---

## Premium Features

### 1. Unlimited Food Logging
Remove the 3 meals per day limit. Log as many meals, snacks, and drinks as you want.

**Why it matters:** Most people eat more than 3 times a day. A coffee, a mid-afternoon snack, a post-dinner treat. Limiting to 3 forces users to either skip logging or combine meals into one entry, which makes the data less useful.

**Value:** This alone justifies the upgrade for anyone who tracks seriously. Inaccurate data leads to inaccurate results.

---

### 2. Unlimited Edits
Edit any logged meal without hitting a daily edit limit. Free users are limited to 3 edits per day.

**Why it matters:** People make mistakes. They log "200g chicken" and realize it was 150g. Or the AI gets a portion wrong. Being unable to fix it means living with bad data all day.

---

### 3. Calorie Bank
Instead of a fixed daily target, you get a weekly calorie budget. Eat less on some days and those saved calories spread to the rest of your week. Eat more on another day and it gets deducted from the remaining days.

Includes daily caps to prevent extreme restriction, a floor that never drops below safe minimums (1,500 for men, 1,200 for women), and automatic macro scaling. The bank resets weekly on your chosen day.

**Why it matters:** Nobody eats the same amount every day. A rigid daily target turns every social dinner or lazy Sunday into a failure. Weekly budgeting matches how the body actually processes calories and removes the guilt from normal eating variation.

**Value:** This is the feature that keeps people using the app long-term. It's the difference between "I went over today, I'm done" and "I went over today, but my week is still on track."

---

### 4. Dynamic Adjustments
The app monitors your weight trend and suggests calorie adjustments when your weight changes by a percentage you choose (3%, 4%, or 5%). As you lose weight, your body needs fewer calories. Dynamic Adjustments catches this before you hit a plateau.

**Why it matters:** Most people set a calorie target once and never update it. Months later they wonder why progress stopped. Their body changed but their target didn't. This closes that gap automatically.

---

### 5. Smart Suggest
AI suggests what to eat next based on your goals, the time of day, and what you've already logged today. Appears as a banner on the Home screen.

**Why it matters:** "What should I eat?" is the hardest question when dieting. Smart Suggest answers it with something that fits your remaining macros and calorie budget.

---

### 6. Pattern Detection
AI analyzes 2-3 weeks of your eating data and detects patterns you might not notice. For example: "On days when your breakfast has less than 20g protein, you tend to exceed your calorie goal by dinner." Each pattern comes with a specific fix.

**Why it matters:** People are terrible at seeing their own habits. The data is there but the pattern is invisible until someone (or something) points it out. This turns raw logging data into behavioral change.

---

### 7. Predictive Warnings
When your intake is trending toward going over your daily target, the app warns you before it happens and suggests lower-calorie alternatives for your next meal.

**Why it matters:** Prevention is easier than damage control. Knowing at 3pm that you're on track to exceed by 400 calories gives you time to adjust dinner. Finding out at 10pm doesn't.

---

### 8. AI Chat Coach
A chat interface where you can ask nutrition questions and get personalized answers based on your actual data. "What should I eat for dinner to hit my protein goal?" or "Why am I always hungry at 4pm?"

**Why it matters:** Generic nutrition advice is everywhere. Personalized advice based on YOUR food log, YOUR goals, and YOUR patterns is what actually moves the needle.

---

### 9. Advanced Analytics
Trend analysis, heatmaps showing your eating patterns across the week, macro distribution charts, consistency scores, and AI-powered analysis. Goes deeper than the basic calorie and macro view.

**Why it matters:** For users who want to optimize, not just track. Advanced analytics shows the difference between "I'm logging" and "I'm learning from my data."

---

### 10. Weekly AI Insights
Every week, the AI reviews your food log and writes a personalized report. It references your specific numbers, food names, and days. It explains why something matters and gives one concrete action for each insight. No generic advice.

When Calorie Bank is on, the insight aligns to your bank cycle day and includes banking pattern analysis.

**Why it matters:** Most people log food but never look back at the data. The weekly insight does the analysis for you and delivers the takeaway in 30 seconds of reading.

**Value:** This is the feature that makes tracking feel like having a nutritionist on retainer. The AI has seen every meal you logged and comments on the patterns you can't see yourself.

---

### 11. Grocery Suggestions
AI generates a personalized shopping list based on your goals and the foods you actually eat. Includes quantities, estimated costs, and a PDF export. Unlocks after you've logged at least 5 days with 7 unique foods (so the list is based on real data, not generic suggestions).

**Why it matters:** The gap between "I know what to eat" and "I bought the right food" is where most diets fail. If the right food isn't in your fridge, you'll eat whatever is. This bridges that gap.

---

### 12. Weight Insights
AI analysis on the Weight Tracker's Insights tab. Examines your weight trend, calculates your actual deficit based on weight change, and explains what the numbers mean in practical terms.

**Why it matters:** Seeing a number on a scale doesn't tell you if you're on track. Weight insights tells you "at your current rate, you'll reach your target in 8 weeks" or "your weight has been flat for 3 weeks, which suggests your calorie target needs adjustment."

---

### 13. Smart Reminders
Context-aware meal reminders that learn when you typically eat and nudge you at the right time. Unlike basic reminders (fixed times), smart reminders adapt to your actual schedule.

**Why it matters:** A breakfast reminder at 8am doesn't help someone who eats at 10am. Smart reminders learn your rhythm and fire when they'll actually be useful.

---

### 14. Smart Adjustment Suggestions
When your weight changes significantly, a card appears on the Home screen suggesting a calorie adjustment. Shows the old target, proposed new target, and why the change is needed. You can accept, modify, or dismiss it.

**Why it matters:** This is the delivery mechanism for Dynamic Adjustments. Instead of silently changing your target, it explains the reasoning and lets you decide. Users who understand why they're changing are more likely to stick with it.

---

### 15. Premium Notification Settings
Additional notification options including pattern-based timing and end-of-day summaries. Goes beyond the basic meal reminders available on the free plan.

**Why it matters:** More control over when and how the app communicates means fewer annoying notifications and more useful ones.

---

### 16. Nutrition Analysis Insights Tab
The full Insights tab inside Nutrition Analysis. Includes goal adherence rings, calorie trend charts, macro split bars, nutrition balance radar, weekly pattern by day of week, meal timing breakdown, top foods, and the AI weekly insight. When Calorie Bank is active, adds bank utilization and daily distribution charts.

**Why it matters:** This is the analytics dashboard for your nutrition. Everything you need to understand your eating patterns in one scrollable view.

## Why Upgrade

The free plan is a calorie tracker. It does the basics well. You log food, you see a number, you try to stay under it.

The premium plan turns that tracker into a system. It learns your habits, adjusts your targets, warns you before you go over, tells you what to eat next, reviews your week, spots patterns you can't see, and gives you the flexibility to eat like a human instead of a machine.

The difference is not more features. The difference is whether the app works for you or you work for the app.
