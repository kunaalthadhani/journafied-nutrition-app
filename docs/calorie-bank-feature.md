# Calorie Bank

## The Simple Version

Your daily calorie target times 7 equals your weekly budget. Eat less on some days, and those saved calories spread to the rest of your week. Eat more on another day, and it gets deducted from the remaining days. As long as your week adds up, you are on track.

No more "I ruined my diet" because of one dinner out.

---

## How It Works

You set a daily target (say 2,000 kcal). That gives you a weekly budget of 14,000 kcal. You pick a start day for your cycle (Monday by default). Every week on that day, the bank resets to zero and you start fresh.

**When you eat less than your target**, the leftover calories go into your bank. If your target is 2,000 and you eat 1,700, you bank 300. Those 300 get spread across the remaining days of your week, so each day's target goes up a little.

**When you eat more than your target**, the extra gets deducted from the remaining days. If you eat 2,300, that's 300 over. The remaining days each drop a little to keep the week balanced.

**Banking and spending only settle at the end of each day.** While you're still eating today, the app shows your banked or surplus amount in real time, but it doesn't adjust future days until tomorrow. This prevents your targets from jumping around mid-day.

**If you don't log for a day**, the app assumes you ate your base target. No banking, no penalty. The bank only moves when there's real data.

---

## The Safety Limits

Three rules protect you from extreme behavior.

**Daily cap.** You choose 15%, 20%, or 25% of your daily target as the maximum you can bank or spend in a single day. On a 2,000 target with a 20% cap, the most you can bank is 400. Even if you eat only 1,000 (1,000 under target), only 400 goes into the bank. This stops extreme restriction from being rewarded.

**Floor.** Your adjusted daily target will never drop below 1,500 kcal for men or 1,200 kcal for women. No matter what the math says, the app will never suggest you eat less than that.

**Ceiling.** Your adjusted target will never exceed your base target plus the cap amount. On a 2,000 base with a 20% cap, the highest target you'll ever see is 2,400. Even with a huge bank balance, one day won't become a binge day.

## A Real Week

Daily target: 2,000 kcal. Weekly budget: 14,000. Cap: 20% (400 max per day).

**Monday.** Eat 1,700. You bank 300. The remaining 6 days each go up by 50.

**Tuesday.** Adjusted target is 2,050. You eat 2,050. Nothing banked, nothing spent. Bank still has 300.

**Wednesday.** Eat 1,800. You bank 200 more. Bank now has 500. Remaining 4 days each go up by 125.

**Thursday.** Adjusted target is 2,125. You eat 2,100. Close enough. Bank stays around 500.

**Friday.** Eat 2,000. Bank still around 500.

**Saturday.** Dinner out. Eat 2,400. That's 400 over base. It gets deducted. Bank drops to about 100.

**Sunday.** Eat 2,000. The 100 remaining in the bank expires at Monday reset.

**Weekly total: 14,050.** Barely over budget. Progress continues. No single day felt like a failure.

## Works For Every Goal

**Losing weight.** This is the main use case. Eat lighter on busy days, use the banked calories for social meals or weekends.

**Gaining weight.** The logic flips. Eating over target on training days banks a surplus. On rest days when appetite is lower, you can eat less without falling behind your weekly gain goal.

**Maintaining.** Same flexibility. Your weight stays stable because the weekly total stays consistent, even though individual days look different.

## What You See In The App

**Home screen calories card.** When bank is on, you see four columns instead of three: Food (what you've eaten), Banked (how much you're under target right now), Surplus (how much you're over), and Remaining (how much you have left today). A green "BANK ON" badge appears next to the title. Tap the small "i" next to Banked or Surplus for a quick explanation.

**Weekly card.** Swipe the calories card to see your weekly view: total used, current bank balance, and remaining weekly budget. A progress bar shows how much of your week's budget is spent. "Day 3 of 7" tells you where you are in the cycle.

**Macros card.** Your protein, carbs, and fat targets adjust automatically with your daily calorie target. If today's adjusted target is 2,100 instead of 2,000, your macro grams go up proportionally. Percentages stay the same.

**Nutrition Analysis insights.** When you open the Insights tab, a Calorie Bank section shows your weekly budget progress, bank balance, utilization percentage (how much of what you banked was actually used), and a day-by-day chart showing what was banked or spent each day.

**Weekly AI insight.** The AI review aligns to your bank cycle day instead of always resetting on Monday. If your cycle starts on Wednesday, the insight covers Wednesday to Tuesday. The AI considers your banking pattern and will comment on whether your distribution is healthy.

**Cycle reset card.** At the start of each new cycle, a card appears on the Home screen showing last week's summary: how much of the budget you used, how many days you logged, how much of your banked calories you actually spent, and how many expired unused. Dismiss it and it won't show again until next reset.

## Settings

Open Settings and tap Calorie Bank. Premium only. You'll see:

**Toggle.** Turn it on or off. Turning it off immediately goes back to the fixed daily target. Any banked calories are lost.

**Cycle start day.** Pick any day of the week. Your bank resets on this day. Changing it mid-cycle resets the bank and starts a new cycle from today.

**Daily cap.** Choose 15%, 20%, or 25%. This is the maximum you can bank or spend in one day. The approximate calorie amount is shown below each option. Both banking and spending use the same cap.

## What Happens When

**You change your goals.** The bank resets immediately. Your remaining cycle uses the new daily target for the days left. This is intentional. A new plan means a fresh start.

**Dynamic Adjustments suggests a change.** If you accept it, the new target waits until your next cycle reset. The current cycle finishes under the old target. This prevents mid-week disruption from automated suggestions.

**You edit a past meal.** The bank recalculates instantly. It derives everything from your actual meal data, not from stored balances. Edit yesterday's lunch and the bank reflects it immediately.

**You close and reopen the app.** The bank recalculates on every app open. If your cycle ended while the app was closed, it archives the completed cycle and starts a new one.

**You sign out.** Premium features are locked. The bank card disappears. Your config stays saved so if you sign back in, it picks up where you left off.

## How It Connects To Other Features

**Pattern detection.** The AI knows when banking is active. It won't flag intentional lighter days as a restriction problem. It only raises a concern if the pattern looks like genuine restrict-then-binge behavior.

**Grocery suggestions.** No change. The grocery list uses your base daily target for weekly calculations. Banking changes when you eat, not what you buy.

**Calendar.** Green means you logged food. Red means you missed a day. Whether you went over or under target doesn't change the color. Both the date bar and the full calendar use the same logic.

**Notifications.** Meal reminders fire based on whether you've logged, not your bank balance.

## Who It's For

Anyone who has ever felt guilty about eating more than their daily target. Anyone who has abandoned a calorie tracker after one "bad" day. Anyone who eats differently on weekdays vs weekends. Anyone who wants the flexibility to enjoy a dinner out without derailing a week of progress.

Calorie banking turns a rigid daily number into a flexible weekly strategy. The science says weekly calorie balance is what drives weight change, not daily. This feature reflects that reality.
